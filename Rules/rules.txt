1. Game State and Components
Game Length: The game is played over exactly 3 battle rounds.


Warbands: Each player commands a warband consisting of a specific set of fighters, their fighter cards, a warscroll, and a 32-card Rivals deck.


Rivals Deck: The deck must be separated into a 12-card Objective deck (gold cards) and a 20-card Power deck (red cards). Power cards are subdivided into Ploys and Upgrades.


The Battlefield: The game board is divided into hexes, which can be starting hexes, edge hexes, blocked hexes, stagger hexes, and empty hexes.


Tokens: The game tracks state using Glory point tokens, Turn tokens, Damage tokens, Move/Charge tokens, Guard/Stagger tokens, and Feature tokens (Treasure/Cover).


Dice: There are two types of dice used. Attack dice contain Critical Attack, Hammer, X Swords, Support, and Double Support symbols. Save dice contain Critical Save, Shield, Dodge, Support, and Double Support symbols.


2. Setup Phase
Muster Warbands: Players reveal their warband choices, place their decks face down, and set up generic tokens on their warscrolls as required by abilities.


Starting Hand: Players shuffle their decks and draw a hand of 3 Objective cards and 5 Power cards. Each player is allowed exactly one "mulligan" (redraw) where they place all Objective and/or Power cards to the side, draw replacements, and shuffle the discarded cards back into the respective decks.


Roll-Offs: When rules require a roll-off, both players roll an Attack die. Critical beats Hammer/Swords; Hammer/Swords beat Support; Support beats blanks. If tied, players roll again.


Determine Territories: Players roll off. The winner chooses the board layout (side of the game board) and picks their territory. The other territory belongs to the loser.


Place Feature Tokens: The loser of the territory roll-off places the first feature token face-down in an empty neutral hex (cannot be a blocked, stagger, or edge hex). Players alternate placing 5 tokens. Tokens cannot be placed in starting, blocked, stagger, edge hexes, or within 2 hexes of another token. If standard placement is impossible, it can be placed in an edge hex. After 5 tokens are placed, there must be at least 1 in each territory. Once all are placed, they are flipped to their numbered (Treasure) side.


Deploy Fighters: Starting with the player who placed the final feature token, players alternate placing 1 fighter into an empty starting hex in their friendly territory until all fighters are deployed.


3. Battle Round Structure
Each of the 3 battle rounds consists of a Combat Phase followed by an End Phase.


Determine First Turn: At the start of each battle round, players roll off. The winner decides who takes the first turn in the combat phase. The loser immediately draws 1 Power card. In battle rounds 2 and 3, ties go to the "underdog" (the player with fewer Glory points).


Combat Phase Sequence: The combat phase consists of players taking alternating turns until each player has taken 4 turns.


Turn Structure: A player's turn consists of an Action step followed by a Power step.


4. The Action Step & Core Abilities
During the Action step, the active player picks exactly one Core Ability to resolve.


Move: Pick a friendly fighter to move into an adjacent empty hex up to their Move characteristic limit. The fighter must end in a different hex than they started. Assign the fighter a Move token.


Attack: Pick a friendly fighter (the attacker), select one of their weapons, and pick a visible enemy fighter within the weapon's range. Resolve the combat sequence.


Charge: Pick a friendly fighter with no Move or Charge tokens. Remove any Guard tokens from them. The fighter resolves a Move ability, receives a Charge token (instead of a Move token), and then must resolve an Attack ability. If a player's fighter has a Charge token, they cannot Move, Attack, or Guard unless all friendly fighters have Charge tokens.


Guard: Pick a friendly fighter and give them a Guard token.


Focus: Discard any number of cards, draw a replacement card of the exact same type for each discarded card, and then draw 1 additional Power card.


5. Combat Sequence
1. Pick Weapon Ability: If the weapon has abilities, the attacker picks 1 to use.


2. Make Attack Roll: Attacker rolls Attack dice equal to the weapon's Dice characteristic.


3. Make Save Roll: Target rolls Save dice equal to the target's Save characteristic.


4. Count Successes:
Attack Successes: Critical hits, symbols matching the weapon's dice symbol (Hammer or X Swords). If the target is Flanked/Surrounded, count Support symbols.


Save Successes: Critical saves, symbols matching the target's save symbol (Shield or Dodge). If the attacker is Flanked/Surrounded, count Support symbols.


Note on Guard: If the target has a Guard token, both Shield and Dodge symbols count as successes, and they cannot be driven back.


Note on Stagger: If the target has a Stagger token, the attacker can re-roll 1 Attack die.


5. Determine Outcome:
Attack Successful: Attack successes > Save successes. Inflict damage equal to the weapon's Damage characteristic. Check if the target is slain. If not slain, they can be driven back.


Drawn Attack: Attack successes = Save successes. The attack fails, but the target can still be driven back.


Failed Attack: Attack successes < Save successes. Nothing happens.


Drive Back: Attacker pushes the target 1 hex away.


Overrun: If Attack criticals > Save criticals, the attacker can push themselves 1 hex into the hex the target was in (after the target is slain or driven back).


Stand Fast: If Save criticals > Attack criticals, the target takes -1 Damage and cannot be driven back.


6. Positioning Modifiers & Visibility
Visibility: Draw a straight line from hex center to hex center. If the line touches/passes through a blocked hex, it is not visible. Fighters do not block visibility.


Flanked: A fighter is Flanked if there is 1 enemy fighter (other than their opponent in the combat) adjacent to them. Grants standard Support successes.


Surrounded: A fighter is Surrounded if there are 2 or more enemy fighters (other than their opponent) adjacent to them. Grants standard Support AND Critical Support successes. A Surrounded fighter is also considered Flanked.


7. Weapon Abilities & Damage
Critical Abilities: Weapon abilities marked with a Critical symbol only trigger if the Attack roll contains 1 or more Criticals.


Specific Abilities:
Grievous: +1 Damage for the Attack.


Brutal: Shield and Dodge do not count as Save successes.


Ensnare: Dodge does not count as a Save success.


Cleave: Shield does not count as a Save success.


Stagger: If successful, give the target a Stagger token.


Grapple: Instead of driving back, the target is pushed 1 hex.


Damage & Slain: Give the target damage tokens equal to the attack/ability's damage. If damage tokens >= Health characteristic, the fighter is slain. Remove the slain fighter, discard their Upgrades, and Uninspire them. The enemy player gains Glory points equal to the slain fighter's Bounty characteristic.


8. The Power Step
After the Action step, players alternate resolving one of the following until both players pass in succession:


Play a Ploy: Discard a Ploy card from the hand and resolve its text immediately.


Play an Upgrade: Equip an Upgrade card to a friendly fighter. The total Glory value of equipped Upgrades across the warband cannot exceed the player's currently held Glory points.


Use a Warscroll Ability: Trigger an ability printed on the warband's card.


Delve: Pick a friendly fighter on a feature token. Flip the token (Treasure becomes Cover, Cover becomes Treasure) and give that fighter a Stagger token. A warband can only use Delve once per Power step.


Pass: Do nothing.


9. The End Phase
Once both players have taken 4 turns, resolve the following in order starting with the player who went first in the combat phase:

Score Objectives: Reveal and score any Objective cards whose conditions are met. Gain the corresponding Glory points and move the card to the scored pile.


Equip Upgrades: Play Upgrades subject to standard restrictions.


Discard: Discard any number of cards from the hand.


Draw Objectives: Draw Objective cards until holding exactly 3.


Draw Power Cards: Draw Power cards until holding exactly 5.


Cleanup: Clear all Move, Charge, Guard, and Stagger tokens from the board. Flip turn tokens face up.


10. Game End and Victory Conditions
In the Final End Phase (end of Battle Round 3), players only resolve Step 1 (Score Objectives) and Step 2 (Equip Upgrades).


The player with the most Glory points wins.


Tiebreakers:


If only one player has fighters remaining, they win.
Highest combined value of held treasure tokens.
Highest total combined Bounty characteristics of surviving fighters.
Draw.
